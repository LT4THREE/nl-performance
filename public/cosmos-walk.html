The Pocket Cosmos Walk

Shrink the Sun to a grapefruit. Walk the Solar System with your own two feet.

Bring a kit:

🍊 grapefruit = Sun · pinhead = Earth · marble = Jupiter · peppercorn = Neptune.
Place the grapefruit where you start. Walk ~600 steps. Drop each object as you're told you've arrived.

How should I track you?

GPS tracks real distance as you walk outdoors. Needs an https page + location permission. Inner planets are close, so expect some wobble from GPS noise.

Now the two you can never walk:

🌟 The next star is ~4,000 km away at this scale — another continent. You'd walk a year and a half, without sleeping, to reach the next pinhead's worth of planets. Between here and there: nothing.

🌌 The Milky Way would span 95 million km — two-thirds of the way to the real Sun. The map of the galaxy doesn't fit inside the galaxy.

In this model, light crawls at 3 cm per second — slower than a snail. The only reason light feels fast is that the universe is monstrously big.
